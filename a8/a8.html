Questions

1. How does the allowed interaction keep the narrative on track? How does it permit variations and branches?

The permitted interaction keeps the narrative on track because you are only able to do actions that will help you towards your goal. However, it seems that the order that you complete actions in order to complete your task does not matter and is up to the player. For example, the second task required me to find five birds and take photos of them. The order in which the birds are found may depend on the player and the different branches they take.

2. Describe the nature of the graphical quality and how it is relevant to the story.

The graphics of the game seem to purposely be pixelated, similar to Minecraft. I believe it is relevant to the story because it gives the story an air of mystery.

3. Describe the nature of the interaction and how it influences choices and how the player feels.

While I was playing the game, I felt very uneasy. The music provided an eerie vibe to the game. Because I felt like something bad was going to happen, I found myself choosing the paths/branches that seemed less scary. For example, during the second task that left me in a dark hallway on a stormy night, I found myself choosing to go through the doors which seemed less scary first. In addition, the fact that there was a very limited number of actions I could take, I often got frustrated because I was stuck and could not figure out what to do next.
